Frequently asked questions

Questions buyers actually ask about payments, land transfer tax and CMHC.

Does the mortgage payment estimate include property tax and utilities?

No. The payment calculator estimates principal and interest only. Property tax, utilities, insurance and condo fees are separate carrying costs. If you want the real monthly ownership number, you need to add those separately.

Why is my bank's payment different from this calculator?

Your lender may use a different rate, payment frequency, compounding assumption, insurance premium, amortization or rounding. Use this as a planning estimate, then confirm the final payment with your mortgage professional.

Do Brampton and Mississauga buyers pay Toronto land transfer tax?

No. Brampton and Mississauga buyers pay Ontario land transfer tax, but they do not pay Toronto municipal land transfer tax unless the property is inside the City of Toronto boundary.

When does Toronto MLTT apply?

Toronto MLTT applies when the property is inside the City of Toronto. Toronto, North York, Scarborough, Etobicoke, East York and York are treated as Toronto areas in this calculator.

Does the first-time buyer rebate remove all land transfer tax?

Not always. The Ontario rebate is up to $4,000. Toronto has a separate first-time buyer rebate of up to $4,475. On many GTA prices, the rebate helps but does not remove the full tax.

Who confirms the final land transfer tax before closing?

Your real estate lawyer confirms the final amount. This calculator is useful for planning, but your lawyer should confirm the exact tax, rebate eligibility and closing statement.

When is CMHC mortgage insurance required?

CMHC mortgage insurance is usually required when the down payment is less than 20 percent on an eligible insured mortgage. The premium protects the lender, not the buyer.

Is CMHC insurance paid upfront?

The premium is usually added to the mortgage instead of being paid fully upfront. In Ontario, the sales tax on the premium is typically a cash closing cost, which surprises many first-time buyers.

What happens if the purchase price is $1.5 million or more?

CMHC insured mortgage rules generally do not apply at $1.5 million and above. Buyers usually need an uninsured mortgage and a larger down payment. Confirm the structure before making an offer.

Can I get a 30-year amortization with less than 20 percent down?

CMHC notes that insured 30-year amortization is available for first-time buyers or new builds. If you are not a first-time buyer and the property is resale, confirm carefully with your mortgage broker.

Should I calculate payment or closing costs first?

Do both before touring. Payment tells you whether the home is comfortable monthly. Closing costs tell you whether you have enough cash to complete the purchase.
